## Slide 2 — Problem Identification

**Observations / Research Findings**
- **2.2 billion** people worldwide live with a vision impairment; the number of blind people is projected to rise from ~43M (2020) to ~**61M by 2050** as populations age.
- Only **28%** of blind and partially sighted people feel able to travel independently — even after training.
- **Indoor navigation is the sharpest unmet need:** unlike outdoors, there are no curbs, walls or traffic sounds to use as landmarks, so an unfamiliar building is often "too difficult to attempt."
- The two existing "physical guidance" options don't scale: a **guide dog costs $45,000–$60,000**, takes 1–5 years, and serves only **~2%** of blind people (<19,000 working dogs worldwide, and falling). The **white cane** only senses below the waist within ~1.2 m and misses head-height hazards — **40%+ of users report head-level accidents monthly.**
- Every existing indoor-navigation product (BlindSquare, GoodMaps, Evelity, Wayfindr) is **audio-only** — **none physically lead** the user.
- Consequences: only **~44%** of blind people are employed (vs 79%), and ~**25%** experience depression, with lost mobility a key driver.

**Problem Statement**
> How might we help a blind person navigate an unfamiliar indoor space **safely and independently** — without a guide dog, a costly wait, or relying on another person?

## Slide 3 — Proposed Solution

**Solution Overview**
**Sam & the Guides** is a system of **three small, low-cost robots + a phone app** that *physically leads* a blind person (Sam) along pre-mapped indoor routes. Two **guide robots** lead the way, a **companion robot** stays beside Sam, and a single piece of **software (the brain)** coordinates all three over Bluetooth. **No camera, no internet.**

**Key Features**
- 🎙️ **Voice destination** — Sam just says "Take me to the store."
- 🧮 **Route + ETA planning** — "be there by 6:00 PM" → the app departs on time by itself.
- 🔗 **Real-time coordination (~5 Hz)** — the hub balances the robots' **speed and spacing** so no one is ever left behind.
- 🛡️ **Safety** — stops on an obstacle, taps the arm, speaks every step; **emergency stop** at any moment.
- 🔒 **Camera-free & offline** — private by design (an advantage in hospitals/care homes).
- 👨‍👩‍👧 **Live family view** — a relative anywhere can follow the journey in real time.

---

## Slide 4 — Objectives & Impact

**Objectives**
- Lead a blind user from point A to point B along a mapped route, reliably.
- Keep the three robots in a **safe formation** — the slowest member (Sam) sets the pace.
- **Stop safely** at obstacles and alert the user (light + arm tap + phone voice).
- Compute **ETA** and support **scheduled departures**.
- Stay **low-cost, camera-free and offline**.
- Provide **live monitoring** for family/caregivers.

**Expected Impact**
- Restores **independent indoor mobility** for a large, ageing, under-served population.
- **Far cheaper and faster** than a guide dog ($45–60k, multi-year wait) — a robotic kit could target **< $2,000**.
- **Differentiated** from every audio-only app: it *physically guides* the user.
- Fits institutional buyers under **accessibility mandates** (European Accessibility Act, ADA) — airports, care homes, museums, hospitals.
- Strong social value: assistive-independence programs return roughly **$4–7 of social value per $1** (SROI) — plus gains in dignity, employment and mental health.
