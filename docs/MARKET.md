# Market Research — Sam & the Guides

## 1. The market (TAM)
Assistive technology for the visually impaired is a real, growing market:

| Metric | Figure |
|---|---|
| Market size (2025) | **~$6–7.4 billion** (estimates range $5.9B–$12.6B by methodology) |
| Growth (CAGR) | **~10–15% / year** |
| Projected (2030–2035) | **$11B–$25B** |

Growth drivers: an ageing population, stronger accessibility regulation, and fast AI-driven product innovation.
*Sources: [The Business Research Company](https://www.thebusinessresearchcompany.com/report/assistive-technologies-for-visually-impaired-global-market-report), [Mordor Intelligence](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/assistive-technologies-for-visually-impaired-market), [Research Nester](https://www.researchnester.com/reports/assistive-technologies-for-visually-impaired-market/8531).*

## 2. The pain we attack (why now)
- **~2.2 billion** people have a vision impairment worldwide ([WHO](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/blindness-and-visual-impairment)).
- A **guide dog costs $30,000–$50,000** and takes **1–5 years** to obtain; **more than half** of candidate dogs never finish training.
- Only about **2% of blind people** ever get a guide dog; roughly **10,000** guide-dog teams exist in the entire US.
- A **white cane** only senses what's immediately in front — no route, no "lead me there."
*Sources: [Service Dogs](https://www.servicedogs.com/statistics/), [IGDF](https://www.igdf.org.uk/about-us/facts-and-figures/), [TotalVet](https://total.vet/service-dog-statistics/).*

→ **Huge unmet demand for an affordable way to be physically guided somewhere.**

## 3. Competitors — and our gap
| Product | What it is | Limitation |
|---|---|---|
| **BlindSquare** | GPS + POI self-voicing app | Audio only, outdoor-focused, no physical guiding |
| **GoodMaps Explore** | Indoor audio wayfinding | Audio only; needs mapped venues |
| **Evelity** | Step-by-step audio in venues | Audio only |
| **Wayfindr** | Open audio-navigation standard | A standard, not a guide |
| **CaBot / AI Suitcase** (CMU/IBM research) | Robotic suitcase that guides | Research prototype, single expensive robot, not on market |

**Everyone on the market is an audio-only phone app.** *Nobody physically leads the user.*
*Sources: [BlindSquare](https://www.blindsquare.com/), [Wayfindr](https://www.wayfindr.net/), [UC Santa Cruz](https://news.ucsc.edu/2024/10/manduchi-wayfinding-apps/).*

## 4. Our differentiation
A **coordinated robot escort** that *physically* leads a blind person and keeps them safe — **no camera, no internet, low-cost hardware**. Cheaper than a guide dog, more than an audio app: it actually walks you there.

## 5. Where it sells first (go-to-market)
Institutions with mapped indoor spaces and a duty of accessibility:
- **Care homes & hospitals** (residents/patients, staff shortages)
- **Airports, train stations, museums, universities, malls**
B2B / institutional pilots first (they own the map and pay), consumer version later.

## 6. One-line positioning
> *"A guide dog costs $50,000 and a 3-year wait. Sam & the Guides gives the same freedom with three small robots and a phone."*
